\## Post 1 — Quantifying Brand Authority in LLM Memory (Personalized PageRank)



Dan Petrovic's original research measuring how deeply a brand is embedded in an LLM's memory, using Personalized PageRank over a directed brand-association graph.



Methodology: prompted Google's Gemini \~200,000 times to name random brands; used top brands as seeds to build an association network of \~3 million names; applied a customized PageRank to score brand authority based on how frequently and centrally the model recalls each brand.



Key insight for content production: brand authority in AI is not popularity — it's how densely a brand sits within the model's association network. Example: Maison Margiela was never recalled unprompted but scored very high because it sits at the dense intersection of many other high-profile fashion brands. Implication: building associations with other authoritative entities in your category matters as much as direct mentions.

\## Post 2 — CAPS: A Content Attribution Payment Scheme for the AI Era



Dan Petrovic's proposed framework (CAPS) addressing the collapse of the web's economic model as AI intermediates content — a micropayment and grounding scheme to attribute and compensate source content used in AI answers.



Relevance: adjacent to content production — addresses the economics of who gets paid when AI uses content, rather than content tactics directly. Included as a forward-looking industry perspective on how content attribution may evolve.
