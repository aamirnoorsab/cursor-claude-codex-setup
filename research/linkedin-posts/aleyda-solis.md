\## Post 1 — Who Owns AI Search Optimization? (Poll, 1,212+ votes)

\## Post 2 — SERP Eye-Tracking Study (sharing MJ Cachón Yáñez / Laika)



Aleyda sharing and analyzing MJ Cachón Yáñez's eye-tracking study measuring the attention-to-action gap for each Google SERP element.



Key findings (data is MJ Cachón's; commentary is Aleyda's):

\- "Black holes of attention" — elements viewed but not clicked: images, product cards, AI Overview Explore Further, PAS, videos, AIO Links (all with a gap ≥68pp). Dwell time 256–561ms indicates scanning, not reading.

\- Most extreme: AIO Explore Further — 83% view it, 0% click it.

\- True converters: Organic (100% engagement, 95.5% CTR) and AIO Main (86% efficiency). These get read, not just looked at.

\- Implication for content production: competing to appear in high-attention/low-click elements yields branding/visibility, not traffic. Know which SERP elements actually convert before optimizing for them.

\## Post 3 — Fan-out Framework (sharing Cyrus Shepard / Zyppy)



Aleyda recommending Cyrus Shepard's 5-step framework for turning AI fan-out queries into better rankings and AI visibility.



The 5 steps:

1\. Find a keyword topic you already rank for

2\. Find fan-out queries

3\. Determine your most important fan-out topics

4\. Optimize for the fan-out queries

5\. Measure the results



Relevance: "Query fan-out" (LLMs running multiple simultaneous queries per prompt) is a recurring AI-search concept. This is a concrete, repeatable content-production process for addressing it.

\## Post 4 — The Winning Global AI Search Strategy Playbook (Aleyda's own framework)



Aleyda's own conference talk (Search 'n Stuff) presenting a framework of 10 AI-readiness characteristics, each assessed via a diagnostic question:



1\. Accessible — Can AI crawlers reach and fetch the relevant pages reliably?

2\. Useful — Does the content solve the user need competitively, better than what's on the first page of AI answers?

3\. Recognizable — Are brand and entity signals explicit (name, category, founder, HQ, funding, product lines) and machine-readable?

4\. Extractable — Are key answers, positioning, and differentiators easy to parse and summarize from the page?

5\. Consistent — Do entity signals match across site, Wikipedia/Wikidata, LinkedIn, review sites, and press?

6\. Corroborated — Do multiple independent third-party sources reinforce the same positioning and claims?

7\. Credible — Do reinforcing sources carry weight (recognized publications, analyst coverage, peer-reviewed or primary data)?

8\. Differentiated — Is the positioning clear, specific, and ownable, or is the language interchangeable with competitors?

9\. Fresh — Is the content recent enough (publish/update dates, current facts, live pricing) to remain credible and citable?

10\. Transactable — Are pricing, plan logic, and feature comparisons clear enough that AI systems can answer "which plan fits my case?"



Talk themes: how search is shifting with AI platforms; how to update search and business metrics to AI impact; how to tie optimization actions to AI-related metrics.



Relevance: The most directly playbook-usable single artifact in the LinkedIn set — a 10-point checklist for producing AI-search-optimized content.
